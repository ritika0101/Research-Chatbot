# Report on Quantum Computing and Cryptography Initiatives in Japan

This report summarizes findings on Japanese advancements in quantum computing and cryptography, highlighting contradictions in reported dates.

**Quantum Cryptography Network and Computing Initiatives:**  Multiple organizations, including NICT, RIKEN, Osaka University, and QunaSys, launched a quantum cryptography network testbed on December 18, 2023. [https://www.nict.go.jp/press/2023/12/18-1.html]  RIKEN also launched a quantum computing cloud service utilizing a domestically produced superconducting quantum computer on March 24, 2023. [https://www.riken.jp/pr/news/2023/20230324_1/index.html]  This research partially utilized funding from the Strategic Innovation Promotion Program (SIP) for advanced quantum technology. [https://www.riken.jp/pr/news/2023/20230324_1/index.html]  The Tokyo QKD network, operational since 2010, serves as a testbed for Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) technology. [https://www.nict.go.jp/press/2023/12/18-1.html]  Quantum cryptography combines QKD for key sharing with one-time pad encryption for absolute security. [https://www.nict.go.jp/press/2023/12/18-1.html] Middleware manages user authentication, program scheduling, and data conversion for quantum computers. [https://www.nict.go.jp/press/2023/12/18-1.html]  A "quantum secure cloud" system, integrating quantum cryptography and secret sharing, is expected to enhance data security across various sectors. [https://www.nict.go.jp/press/2023/12/18-1.html]


**Contradictions:** The provided URL for the press release regarding the quantum cryptography network testbed (https://www.nict.go.jp/press/2025/03/13-1.html) lists dates in 2023 and 2025, creating a contradiction.  The discrepancy lies in the year of the press release itself (2025) conflicting with the reported launch date of the network (2023).  This requires further investigation to clarify the accurate launch date.
