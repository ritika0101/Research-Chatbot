# Goods and Services Tax (GST) Report
## Overview

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax introduced in India on July 1, 2017, replacing a range of pre-existing taxes like VAT, service tax, central excise duty, entertainment tax, and octroi [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goods_and_Services_Tax_(India)].

## Key Features

* GST is a comprehensive, multistage, and destination-based tax [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goods_and_Services_Tax_(India)].
* It unified the country’s tax structure, simplifying the taxation of goods and services and eliminating the need for multiple taxes previously levied by both central and state governments [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goods_and_Services_Tax_(India)].

## GST Reforms

The GST reforms introduced in September 2025 represent a landmark step in reshaping India’s taxation system to better serve the aspirations of its youth [https://www.pib.gov.in/FactsheetDetails.aspx?Id=150302].

* GST on leather, footwear, textiles, handicrafts, and toys reduced to 5% to boost youth-led MSMEs and exports [https://www.pib.gov.in/FactsheetDetails.aspx?Id=150302].
* Essential learning materials like pencils, erasers, and exercise books made GST-free to ease education costs [https://www.pib.gov.in/FactsheetDetails.aspx?Id=150302].

## Impact of GST on Businesses

The launch of the GST system in July 2017 was one of the pivotal moments in the economic history of independent India [https://cleartax.in/s/impact-of-gst-on-business].

* GST has simplified the indirect tax system with a uniform tax structure and a centralized tax collection process for the whole nation [https://cleartax.in/s/impact-of-gst-on-business].
