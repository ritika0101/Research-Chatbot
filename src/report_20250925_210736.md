# GST Reforms Report
## Overview

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) reforms, introduced in September 2025, aim to simplify India's taxation system, promoting affordability, healthier lifestyles, and improved ease of living for youth and households.

## Key Takeaways

* GST on **leather, footwear, textiles, handicrafts, and toys** reduced to **5%** [1].
* **Essential learning materials** like pencils, erasers, and exercise books made **GST-free** [2].
* GST on **gyms/fitness centres** slashed from **18% to 5%** [1].
* GST on **two-wheelers (≤ 350cc) and small cars** cut from **28% to 18%**, enhancing youth mobility [1].
* **Healthcare** made affordable with GST on medicines cut to **5%/Nil**, while **health insurance is made exempted from GST** [1].

## Impact

The GST reforms are expected to:
* Reduce household bills by **13%** for groceries and daily essentials [2].
* Save small car buyers around **₹70,000** [2].
* Empower MSMEs and boost economic growth.

## Conclusion

The GST reforms of 2025 mark a significant step towards a more simplified and affordable taxation system in India, targeting key sectors that impact the youth and common households.
